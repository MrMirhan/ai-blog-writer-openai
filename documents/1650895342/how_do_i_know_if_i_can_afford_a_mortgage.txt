- 5. Closing costs: 


There are certain costs associated with the purchase or sale of a home. These costs are generally referred to as "closing costs." 

Closing costs are typically incurred by the buyer and the seller. The buyer typically pays the costs associated with the loan, such as the origination fee, the mortgage insurance, and the points. The seller typically pays the costs associated with the sale, such as the real estate commission and the title insurance. 

However, the parties can agree to split some of the costs. For example, the buyer may agree to pay the seller's closing costs. 

The amount of closing costs can vary depending on the state, the property, and the parties involved. However, they typically range from 2% to 5% of the purchase price.
