What affects your credit card score? 


- 1. Factors that affect your credit score: 


There are many factors that go into calculating your credit score. The most important factor is your credit utilization ratio, which is the percentage of your credit limit that you are using. If you have a $1,000 credit limit and you have a balance of $200, your utilization ratio is 20%. The lower your ratio, the better. Other factors that affect your score include your payment history, your credit mix, and the age of your credit history.

- 2. How to improve your credit score: 


There are a few ways that you can improve your credit score. The most important way to improve your credit score is to make sure that you are always paying your bills on time. You should also try to keep your credit utilization low, and make sure that you have a good mix of credit accounts. You can also improve your credit score by dispute any inaccurate information on your credit report.
