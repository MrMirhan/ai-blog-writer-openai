- 1. What is a credit score? 


A credit score is a three digit number that lenders use to help them decide how likely you are to repay a loan. The score is based on your credit history, which is a record of how you've repaid debts in the past. 

Your credit score affects how much you'll pay for a loan, the interest rate you'll get, and even whether you can get a loan at all. Lenders use your credit score to help them decide how much money to loan you, and how much interest to charge.

- 2. Factors that influence a credit score 


Credit scores are determined by a variety of factors, including how much debt you have, how timely you pay your bills, and how long you have had credit.

Your credit utilization ratio is also a big factor in your credit score. This is the percentage of your total credit limit that you are currently using. If you have a high credit utilization ratio, it can indicate that you are overextended and may be at risk of defaulting on your loans.

Your credit score can also be affected by your credit history. The longer you have had credit and the more consistently you have paid your bills on time, the better your credit score will be.

There are a number of other factors that can influence your credit score, including your age, your race, and your sex. However, these factors are not as important as your credit history and your credit utilization ratio.
