How can you protect your credit score from fraud

There are a few different ways that credit score fraud can happen. One way is when someone gets ahold of your personal information, like your Social Security number or your driver’s license number, and uses it to open up new credit accounts in your name. They might also use your information to take out loans or make purchases that you never authorized.

Another way that credit score fraud can happen is when someone gets ahold of your credit report and changes the information on it. They might change your credit score, your credit history, or your credit limit. This can make it look like you have a lot more debt than you actually do, or like you’ve missed a lot of payments. This can make it harder for you to get approved for new credit accounts or loans.
