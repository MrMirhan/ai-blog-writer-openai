How to maintain a high credit scoreWhat is a credit score? 


Your credit score is a three digit number that lenders use to determine how risky it is to loan you money. The higher your credit score, the lower the risk to the lender, and the more likely you are to get a loan at a good interest rate. 

Your credit score is based on your credit history, which is a record of how you've repaid your debts in the past. Lenders look at things like how often you've missed payments, how much you owe compared to how much credit you have available, and how long you've had credit accounts. 

There are a number of different credit scoring models, but the most common one is the FICO score. FICO scores range from 300 to 850, and most people have a score in the 600 to 750 range.

How to maintain a high credit score 



The following are a few tips to help you maintain a high credit score:

1. Pay your bills on time. This is the most important factor in determining your credit score.

2. Keep your credit utilization low. Try to keep your credit utilization below 30% of your total credit limit.

3. Don’t open too many accounts at once. Opening too many accounts at once can negatively affect your credit score.

4. Don’t close old accounts. Closing old accounts can negatively affect your credit score.

5. Monitor your credit score. Monitor your credit score regularly to make sure it stays in good shape.
