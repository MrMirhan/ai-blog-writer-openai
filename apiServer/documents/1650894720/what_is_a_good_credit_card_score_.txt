- 1. What is a credit score? 


A credit score is a three-digit number that is used to represent a borrower’s credit risk. Lenders use credit scores to help them decide whether to extend credit to a borrower and at what interest rate.

Credit scores are calculated based on information in a borrower’s credit report. The most important factors in a credit score are a borrower’s payment history, credit utilization, length of credit history, new credit and type of credit.

Credit scores range from 300 to 850. The higher the credit score, the less risky the borrower is to the lender. Lenders typically require a credit score of 620 or higher to approve a mortgage.

There are a number of different credit scoring models, but the most common is the FICO score. The FICO score is used by 90% of lenders.
