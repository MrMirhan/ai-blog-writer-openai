What affects your credit card score?


1. Factors that affect a credit score: 



There are five factors that affect a credit score: payment history, utilization, length of credit history, new credit and types of credit. 

1. Payment history: This is the most important factor, and it accounts for 35% of your credit score. It looks at how often you've paid your bills on time. Late payments and missed payments will hurt your score.

2. Utilization: This accounts for 30% of your credit score. It looks at how much of your available credit you're using. If you have a $1,000 credit limit and you have a balance of $500, your utilization is 50%. A high utilization can hurt your score.

3. Length of credit history: This accounts for 15% of your credit score. It looks at how long you've had credit accounts. The longer you've had credit, the better your score will be.
